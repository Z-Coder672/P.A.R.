About P.A.R.

P.A.R. is a robot that draws 37x18 monochrome pixel art by flipping squisks (square discs).

It was designed to provide a unique art experience, letting the artist watch their art get made physically, through an interesting process.

FAQ

What does P.A.R. stand for?

P.A.R. stands for Pixel Art Robot.

How much does it cost to submit art to P.A.R.??

Submitting art to P.A.R. is completely free. There are no fees or ads.

How does P.A.R. work?

P.A.R. is basically a CNC machine with a special toolhead. It has a color sensor, a servo motor, and a pin, or pusher. The robot first scans the grid with the color sensor. Then it grabs the next pixel art piece from the server. Once it has the art, it starts flipping squisks. Here's an animation of how a squisk is flipped:

Next, it rescans the grid to check which squisks it failed to flip. It finally fixes any errors it finds.

Can I build it myself?

Yes! If you want to subject yourself to weeks or months of pain and suffering, you can access the full CAD model for free, and 3D print all the parts. You'll also need all of the items on this material list. You can find the code on my GitHub.
